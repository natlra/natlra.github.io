Background

I was born in Paris, France, and spent my early childhood moving between Europe and the United States before settling permanently in Colorado. Growing up across multiple cultures and environments developed adaptability, communication skills, and an ability to integrate quickly into new teams—traits that continue to influence how I approach engineering projects and collaboration.

Professional Discipline

Prior to engineering, I worked in high-end restaurants in Boulder, including Pizzeria Locale (now Pizzeria Alberico), where I served as a Garde Manger chef. Working alongside chefs affiliated with Frasca Food and Wine instilled a strong respect for process discipline, consistency, and attention to detail. This environment reinforced the importance of preparation, accountability, and executing under pressure—principles I now apply directly to technical and academic work.

Motorsports & Engineering Outside the Classroom

I actively build and campaign a drift car, currently undergoing an LS engine swap in collaboration with an experienced mechanic in Loveland. Beyond driving, I am directly involved in mechanical assembly, troubleshooting, wiring, and system integration. Track driving has sharpened my understanding of vehicle dynamics, failure modes, and rapid problem-solving in real-world conditions. My long-term goal is to compete consistently while continuing to deepen my engineering skill set.
